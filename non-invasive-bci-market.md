<p><p>The non-invasive Brain-Computer Interface (BCI) market comprises various technologies for monitoring brain activity. EEG (Electroencephalography) measures electrical signals and is widely used for its cost-effectiveness. fMRI (Functional Magnetic Resonance Imaging) offers high spatial resolution for brain activity mapping but is expensive. NIRS (Near-Infrared Spectroscopy) uses light to monitor hemodynamic responses, suitable for portable applications. MEG (Magnetoencephalography) detects magnetic fields generated by neural activity, providing excellent temporal resolution. Lastly, ECoG (Electrocorticography), while invasive, offers high fidelity, yet itâ€™s less commonly used for standard BCI applications.</p></p>
