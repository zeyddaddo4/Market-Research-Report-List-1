<p><p>The battery raw materials market comprises three primary types: anode, cathode, and separator materials. Anodes are typically made from graphite or silicon, facilitating electron flow during discharge. Cathodes, often composed of lithium compounds, determine energy density and overall battery performance. Separators, made from polymers like polyethylene or polypropylene, prevent short circuits by allowing ions to pass through while keeping the anode and cathode apart. Together, these components are crucial for the efficiency and safety of battery technologies in various applications.</p></p>
