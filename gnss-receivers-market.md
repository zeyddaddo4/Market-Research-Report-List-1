<p><p>The GNSS receivers market consists of single-frequency and multi-GNSS receivers. Single-frequency GNSS receivers utilize signals from a single frequency, typically leading to lower cost and simplicity, making them suitable for basic applications like personal navigation. In contrast, multi-GNSS receivers can access multiple satellite constellations, enhancing accuracy, reliability, and positioning capabilities across diverse environments. This versatility caters to advanced applications such as surveying, autonomous vehicles, and precision agriculture, driving demand for multi-GNSS technology in various industries.</p></p>
