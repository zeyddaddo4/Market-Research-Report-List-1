<p><p>The industrial hydrogen market consists of two main types: captive hydrogen and merchant hydrogen. Captive hydrogen refers to hydrogen produced on-site by companies for their own use, primarily in processes like refining or ammonia manufacturing. In contrast, the merchant hydrogen market involves the production and distribution of hydrogen sold to various clients, often through pipelines or trucks. This market caters to a broader range of industries, including transportation and fuel cells, enabling flexibility in supply and demand.</p></p>
